Preserving 3,000 Years of Art

From ancient mosaics to contemporary Tunisian masterpieces.

The Museum Story

Tunisia’s museums are world-renowned for housing the largest collection of Roman mosaics. The Bardo National Museum, located in a 19th-century Beylical palace, stands as a symbol of our diverse heritage—merging Punic, Roman, Islamic, and Modern eras.

Featured Collections

Roman Mosaics

Intricate floor art from Carthage and El Jem, depicting mythology and daily life.

Islamic Ceramics

Exquisite pottery and "Kallaline" tiles from the golden age of the Medina.

Modern School of Tunis

20th-century paintings that capture the light and colors of Tunisian streets.

Plan Your Visit

Location: Le Bardo, Tunis / Carthage / Sousse

Hours: Tuesday – Sunday, 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM

Entry: 13 TND for international visitors / 8 TND for locals.
